Every page — Projects, Ops, Dashboard — has a ⚙ Settings button in the lower-left, plus quick links to jump between views.

Step 1

Create a Project

Click + New Project in the top-right corner and give it a name — usually the video title.

1
Click "+ New Project"
Top-right header button. Type the project name and press Enter.
2
Sub-projects for phases
Right-click any project in the sidebar to add a child — useful for Pre-Production and Post-Production phases.
3
Mark complete when done
Right-click a project and choose Complete to move it to the Completed section at the bottom of the sidebar. Keeps your active list clean.
Step 2

Add People in Settings

All team members live in one global directory. Open ⚙ Settings (lower-left on every page) and switch to the People tab.

➕
Add a person
Type a name, optionally an email and role, then click + Add. They're now available for every project.
✉
Connect an email
Edit the email field on any person row. When you export a meeting to Outlook, anyone with an email is added automatically to the To: line.
🔀
Merge duplicates
Use the Merge Duplicates picker to fold one name into another. Every roster, task, and attendee entry is reassigned automatically.
🗑
Delete a person
The ✕ on a person row removes them from every project roster, unassigns their tasks, and clears their email. Irreversible.
Step 3

Assign a Roster to the Project

Open the Schedule Tools panel at the bottom, then click the Role Assignees tab. Each project picks its team from the global directory.

➕
Click a suggestion to assign
Dashed "+ Name" chips list everyone available for each role. Click to add them to this project.
✕
Click an assigned chip to unassign
Solid chips are on the project. Click to remove them from this project's roster (they stay in the directory).
⚙
Manage People shortcut
The ⚙ Manage People button at the top of the panel jumps straight into Settings → People if you need to add, edit, or merge someone globally.

Step 3 continued

Creating & Editing Tasks

1
Drag a role chip onto a day
A panel slides up. Choose the Person from the dropdown (populated from Role Assignees + anyone previously assigned), add a task name, and set the Effort level.
2
Drag tasks to move them
Grab any task on the calendar and drop it on a new day. Hold Alt while dragging to duplicate it.
3
Resize for multi-day tasks
Drag the right edge of a task to stretch it across multiple days. The bar stays unbroken across the week.
Step 3 continued

Reviews, Shoots & Key Dates

Red chips create event blocks. Gray chips mark Key Dates. Both work differently from regular tasks.

1
Three key date types
🏁 Project Due Date — final deadline. 🎯 Milestone — internal checkpoint. 📦 Delivery — asset or deliverable hand-off. All appear as gray date markers.
2
Add attendees by dropping role chips onto the event
Drop any role or People chip onto a red block or gray Key Date to add an attendee. Their name badge appears on the block.
3
Switch event type by clicking the badge
Click the colored label badge on any event (e.g. "Review") to convert it — Review, Studio Shoot, On-Location Shoot, Project Due Date, Milestone, or Delivery.

Dashboard

The Personal Dashboard

Click 📋 Dash in the lower-left nav. Select a person on the left, and their day is laid out on the right — tailored for the individual.

📅
Today & Coming Up
What's on this person's plate today, plus a rolling look at the next few days. Click any project title to jump back into the schedule.

🔎
Date picker + search
Jump to any date with the header picker, or filter the sidebar with the search box to find a teammate quickly.

Step 4 continued

Export Tab Details

📝
Text
Formatted plain text. Copy writes real bold for Word/PowerPoint and ** markers for Slack/Notion simultaneously.
📊
Table / Excel
Tab-separated values. Copy as TSV, or select all in the table and paste directly into Excel.
🖼
PNG
Downloads a high-resolution image of the next 4 weeks (or longer if tasks extend beyond that).
✅
Trello Export
Copies a checklist of producer categories to paste into Trello or Planyway. Tasks are automatically routed to Pre-Production, Producing, or Post-Producing.
Step 4 continued

Outlook Invites

Send a real Outlook meeting with the full roster in the To: line. Every review, shoot, kickoff, and key date shows up here automatically.

🌐
Invite Web — open in Outlook Web
Click 🌐 Open next to any meeting. A new tab pre-fills the subject, time, location (project name), and To: line with every attendee who has an email on file.
📅
Invites — download .ics
Download a single .ics file or all of them at once. Each event includes ATTENDEE entries for people with emails, so Outlook adds them as proper invitees on import.
✉
Missing someone on the To: line?
Open ⚙ Settings → People and add their email. Next export picks it up automatically.

Step 4 continued

Role Assignees Tab

The Role Assignees tab is where you pick this project's team from the global directory. It's project setup, not export.

➕
Click a "+ Name" chip to assign
Dashed chips list everyone available for each role. One click adds them to this project.
✕
Click an assigned chip to remove
Removes them from this project's roster only — their directory entry and other projects are untouched.
🎯
Roster drives the palette
Anyone on the roster appears as a People chip in the palette — drag a chip onto any day to assign them to a task in one move.
Settings

The Settings Menu

The ⚙ Settings button sits in the lower-left on every page. It has two tabs.

💾
Data — Backup & Restore
Export Data downloads a JSON snapshot of every project, name history, and saved emails. Import Data merges a snapshot back in (projects with matching IDs are overwritten).
👥
People — Directory
One row per person, showing their name, email, and the roles they've been used in. Add, edit emails inline, delete globally, or merge duplicates into a single entry.
Tips

Managing Projects

⋯
Right-click any project for options
Rename, Add Subproject, Duplicate, Complete, or Delete. Deleting a project removes all its subprojects too.
✓
Complete moves projects to a separate section
Completed projects move to a Completed section at the bottom of the sidebar, collapsed by default. Right-click to reopen or delete.
⎇
Alt + drag to duplicate
Hold Alt and drag a project in the sidebar to create a full copy — including all tasks, subprojects, and attendees.
